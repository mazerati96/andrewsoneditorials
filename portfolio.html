Example 01

Opening scene — fantasy novel

Fantasy Line Editing
Original
The forest was dark and it was very quiet. Mara walked through the trees carefully. She was scared. There were sounds coming from somewhere and she didn't know what they were. She kept walking even though she really didn't want to. The trees were tall and they blocked out the sky so it was hard to see. She held her lantern up high to try and see better but it didn't really help that much.
📋
Editor's notes: Repetitive sentence structure (Subject + Verb, Subject + Verb). Telling rather than showing emotion ("She was scared"). Weak adverbs ("carefully", "really"). Lacks sensory immersion and narrative momentum.
Edited
The forest swallowed sound. No wind. No birdsong. Only the soft crunch of Mara's boots and the hammering of her own pulse. She moved between the trunks with her lantern held high, its pale circle of light barely reaching the undergrowth. Something rustled — left, maybe, or deeper in — and she stopped breathing. Kept walking anyway.
✏️
What changed: Varied sentence rhythm to build tension. Replaced telling with physical, sensory detail to show fear. Cut redundant adverbs. Used fragmentation to mirror the character's anxious state of mind.
Example 02

Character introduction — YA contemporary

Young Adult Developmental Editing
Original
My name is Jasmine and I'm sixteen years old. I have brown hair and brown eyes and I'm kind of average looking. I go to Westbrook High School. My best friend is called Priya and she's really pretty and outgoing unlike me. I've always been kind of shy. My parents are divorced and I live with my mom. I like reading and music and I don't really like parties or being around a lot of people.
📋
Editor's notes: Classic "character dumping" — listing facts instead of revealing character through action or voice. Physical description is generic. No narrative hook. The character's internal world is flat. Readers need to be intrigued, not briefed.
Edited
The thing about being invisible is you see everything. I've been watching Priya navigate the cafeteria for three years — the way she laughs too loud near the seniors' table, how her smile goes a little tighter whenever Jake Chen walks past — and she has no idea. That's the deal with best friends who are beautiful. They forget you have eyes. I pull my earbuds in tighter and open my book. Westbrook High on a Monday morning is not something I need to experience with full sensory capacity.
✏️
What changed: Opened with voice and a compelling observation instead of a fact-list. Revealed character traits (perceptive, introverted, dry wit) through action and thought. Introduced the best-friend dynamic with implied conflict and created immediate intrigue without a single biographical bullet point.
Example 03

Dialogue scene — contemporary romance

Romance Copy Editing
Original
"I didn't mean it like that." He said, not looking at her.

"Well that's how it came across," Maya replied back to him angrily.

He sighed loudly. "Can we just drop it".

"No we can't just drop it, Caleb. You said what you said." She crossed her arms across her chest and looked away from him out of the window.
📋
Editor's notes: Punctuation error on first dialogue tag (capital "He" after comma). Redundant phrases ("replied back to him", "crossed her arms across her chest"). Weak adverb ("angrily" — show it). Missing question mark. Clunky final sentence.
Edited
"I didn't mean it like that," he said, not looking at her.

"Well, that's how it came across." Maya's jaw tightened.

He sighed. "Can we just drop it?"

"No, we can't just drop it, Caleb. You said what you said." She crossed her arms and turned to the window.
✏️
What changed: Fixed dialogue punctuation throughout. Removed "replied back" (redundant) and "loudly" (unnecessary). Replaced "angrily" with a physical beat that shows Maya's emotion. Tightened the final sentence by cutting redundant phrasing.
